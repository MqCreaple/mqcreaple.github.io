The world gives us a very special power, which none of the ancients could ever imagine -- the search engine. The search engine can tell you the answer to every question you wanna ask, and give you the latest result from scientists and authorities.

In this article, I will show some basic techniques and tricks to use the search engine, especially Google.

One Principle -- search keywords, not sentences

Search Engine is not your secretary. Google cannot understand complex sentences, but only keywords or short sentences.

For example, if you want to know how many people are there in China, you should search:

China population

Now, search engines have become smarter, and they can understand short questions. Actually, you can also search like this, but not the way you should use google: 

How many people are there in China?
[[google-search-1.jpg]]

However, if you do this, google won't understand you:

[[google-search-2.jpg]]

Turn off the Light -- Minus Symbol '-'

There might be multiple results for one keyword, and most of them are not what you want. In this case, you should add other words to describe what you want precisely.

However, sometimes you might not know what something is, but you know what it isn't. Instead of turn on more lights to find it, you should turn off the light.

I have a friend whose name is Jason Zhang, and I would like to search him on google. However, most of the search result is about a singer, which is not I want.

[[google-search-3.jpg]]

Therefore, I exclude the tag 'singer' while I am searching.

Jason Zhang -singer

Then I get another Jason Zhang who is a CEO.
And another Jason Zhang who is a Ph.D.
And another Jason Zhang who is an analyst.
And a musician Jason Zhang.
And, lots and lots of Jason Zhangs...... 

Jason Zhang -singer -CEO -PhD -university -analyst -music -engineer
[[google-search-4.jpg]]

At last, I still cannot find my friend Jason Zhang...╮(╯▽╰)╭

Put Anything You Want Here -- Star Symbol '*'

While matching with text in Google's database, the star symbol '*' can match any words.

For example, the following pattern:

the most * thing you've done

can match these results:

  • the most interesting thing you've done
  • the most shameless thing you've done
  • the most impressive thing you've done
  • the most difficult thing you've done
  • ...

And also some more intelligent results:

  • the best thing you've done
  • the smartest thing you've done
  • the craziest thing you've done
  • the weirdest thing you've ever done
  • ...
[[google-search-5.jpg]]

Relationship Between Words -- AND OR

The default relationship between every words you typed in the searching area is AND. This means that the search engine would find contents which contains every words.

For example, if you say:

China US population

The search results would not only contain population of China, but also population of the US.

[[google-search-6.jpg]]

This is same as:

China AND US population

Of course, there is another kind of relationship -- OR. You can also search: 

China OR US population

This time, the search result would contain either the population of China, or the population of the US. You cannot see any pages contain both these two numbers.

[[google-search-7.jpg]]

CONCLUSION

  1. One Principle: search keywords, not sentences
  2. Use Minus Symbol - to exclude one word from the result
  3. Use Star Symbol * to match any words or phrases
  4. Use AND OR to claim the relationship between words
